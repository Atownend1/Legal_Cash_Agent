UK Legal Compliance: A Critical Difference

SRA Compliance Requirements

The Solicitors Regulation Authority (SRA) has specific requirements for legal technology, particularly around client money handling and financial reporting. Here's how each platform addresses these:

Yieldly's UK Compliance

✅
SRA Accounts Rules Compliance

Built-in compliance with SRA Accounts Rules 2019, including client money segregation and reporting requirements.

✅
UK Banking Integration

Direct integration with major UK banks (Barclays, HSBC, Lloyds, NatWest) for real-time transaction monitoring.

✅
HMRC Connectivity

Ready-to-use HMRC integration for VAT reporting and Making Tax Digital compliance.

✅
UK Data Protection

GDPR-compliant data handling with UK-based servers and data processing.

Ayora.ai's Compliance Limitations

❌
US-Focused Compliance

Primarily designed for US legal compliance (ABA Model Rules, state bar requirements) with limited UK adaptations.

❌
Manual SRA Setup

Requires extensive customisation and manual configuration to meet SRA requirements, often needing external consultants.

⚠️
Limited UK Banking

Basic UK bank connectivity available but lacks deep integration with UK-specific banking features.

❌
No HMRC Integration

No built-in HMRC connectivity, requiring third-party solutions for UK tax compliance.

Impact on UK Firms

For UK law firms, compliance isn't optional—it's essential. The SRA can impose severe penalties for non-compliance, including:

  • Financial penalties up to £25,000
  • Practice closure in severe cases
  • Individual solicitor suspensions
  • Reputational damage and client loss

Yieldly's built-in UK compliance provides peace of mind and reduces regulatory risk, while Ayora.ai's US focus creates additional compliance burdens for UK firms.
